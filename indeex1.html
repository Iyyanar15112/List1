  1. History



    1. Background

      2. The Indian cricket team has a rich history, evolving from its introduction by European sailors in the 18th century to becoming a global powerhouse. Initially, the team struggled, taking nearly 20 years to secure its first Test victory.

    2. Foundation

      3. The foundation of a cricket team involves several key aspects: establishing a governing body, defining player roles and responsibilities, and fostering a positive team environment. The governing body, like the BCCI in India, sets rules, organizes matches, and manages the overall structure of the sport.

    3. Expansion and termination

      4. Cricket team expansion and termination are common occurrences, often driven by factors like financial viability, talent development, and market demand.

  3. Organisation

    1. Tournament format

      2. The knockout format is simple—teams play matches where the loser is eliminated, and the winner advances. In a round-robin format, each team plays against all other teams, and points are awarded for wins or ties. A double round-robin is similar but teams play each other twice.

    2. Player acquisition, squad composition and salaries

      3. player acquisition happens through auctions or drafts, where teams bid for players within a set budget. Squad composition rules often include a limit on overseas players and a minimum number of local or uncapped players.

    3. Match rules

      4. Test, ODI, or T20. Each team gets one or two innings depending on the format. In ODIs, each team plays 50 overs; in T20s, it's 20 overs. Bowlers have limits on overs they can bowl, and batsmen aim to score as many runs as possible. A team wins by scoring more runs than the opponent. Fielding restrictions, powerplays, DRS (Decision Review System), and rules for no-balls and wides apply. In case of tied scores, Super Overs are used in limited-overs matches

    4. Prize money

      5. prize money is awarded to the winning and runner-up teams, with the champion team getting the highest share. Other awards include cash for Player of the Match, Player of the Tournament, Best Bowler, Best Batter, and more

  4. Teams

    1. Current teams

      2. 10 teams: Chennai Super Kings (CSK), Mumbai Indians (MI), Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB), Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR), Rajasthan Royals (RR), Sunrisers Hyderabad (SRH), Delhi Capitals (DC), Punjab Kings (PBKS), Gujarat Titans (GT), and Lucknow Super Giants (LSG).

    2. Defunct teams

      3. These include Deccan Chargers, who won the IPL in 2009 but were terminated in 2012; Kochi Tuskers Kerala, removed after just one season in 2011 due to contractual disputes; Pune Warriors India, which withdrew in 2013 over financial issues; Rising Pune Supergiant and Gujarat Lions, which were temporary replacements during the 2016 and 2017 seasons when CSK and RR were suspended

    3. Timeline

      4. In 2011, the league expanded to 10 teams but later saw some teams terminated. In 2016–2017, Gujarat Lions and Rising Pune Supergiant played temporarily during the suspension of CSK and RR. The IPL returned to 8 teams in 2018, and by 2022, it expanded again to 10 teams with the addition of Gujarat Titans and Lucknow Super Giants.

  5. Tournament seasons and results

    1. Performance in the IPL by title

      2. Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR) have won twice, while Rajasthan Royals (RR), Sunrisers Hyderabad (SRH), and Deccan Chargers have each won once. Newer teams like Gujarat Titans made a strong impact by winning the title in their debut season in 2022

    2. IPL season results

      3. Chennai Super Kings (CSK) and Mumbai Indians (MI) have dominated the league, with CSK winning in 2010, 2011, 2018, 2021, and 2023, while MI claimed titles in 2013, 2015, 2017, 2019, and 2020

  6. Teams performance

    1. League stage positions

      2. Finishing in the top two provides an extra chance to reach the final through Qualifier 1. Positions are often closely contested, with net run rate (NRR) playing a key role in tie-breaks.
